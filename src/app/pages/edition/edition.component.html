Artwork description:


This piece is based on a public domain image of Gordon Isaacs, who as a baby became the first patient treated with a linear accelerator for a retinoblastoma. Gordon's right eye had been removed on January 11 1957 to try to arrest the spread of cancer. His left eye, however, had a localized tumour that prompted Dr. Henry Kaplan, the pioneering oncologist, to attempt to treat it with radiation therapy.

Mr. Isaacs, at the time of writing, is still with us and has perfect vision in his left eye.

Dr. Kaplan, despite never having smoked, died of lung cancer in 1984. Kaplan first became interested in oncology because his father was killed by a lung cancer.

Not all mad scientists are insane scientists.
